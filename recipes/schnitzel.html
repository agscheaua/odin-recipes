Schnitzel

Description:

A schnitzel is a thin slice of meat that is breaded and fried,

ntil it becomes golden, crispy on the outside, and tender inside.

Ingredients:

  • 4 pieces of thinly sliced meat (traditionally pork, chicken, or veal)
  • ½ cup flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper
  • Oil or butter for frying
  • Optional: lemon slices and parsley for serving

Steps:

    Prepare the Meat:

  1. Place each piece of meat between two sheets of plastic wrap.
  2. Gently pound it with a meat mallet (or rolling pin) until about ½ cm thin.
  3. Season both sides with salt and pepper.

    Prepare the Breading Station:

  1. Put flour in one shallow plate.
  2. Beat eggs in a second plate.
  3. Put breadcrumbs in a third plate.

    Bread the Schnitzels:

  1. Dredge each piece of meat in flour (shake off excess).
  2. Dip it into the egg mixture.
  3. Coat it with breadcrumbs, pressing lightly so they stick.

    Fry the Schnitzels:

  1. Heat about 1 cm of oil or butter in a large pan over medium-high heat.
  2. When hot, add schnitzels and fry for 2–3 minutes per side, until golden brown.
  3. Place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.

    Serve:

  1. Serve hot with lemon slices on top.
  2. Common sides: mashed potatoes, fries, salad, or rice.

Appendix:

1 tbsp = 15g

1 cup = 340g
