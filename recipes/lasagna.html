Lasagna

Description:

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of pasta,

rich meat or vegetable sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese,

and melted mozzarella, all baked to golden perfection.

Ingredients:

    For the meat sauce:

  • 500g ground beef(or mix beef & pork)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2–3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 400g tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp pepper, 1 tsp dried oregano (optional: basil, thyme)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

    For the béchamel sauce:

  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 3 tbsp flour
  • 750ml milk
  • Salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg

    Other:

  • 9–12 lasagna sheets (no-boil or pre-cooked as needed)
  • 200–300g mozzarella (or a mix with parmesan)
  • Optional: ricotta or cottage cheese layer

Steps:

    Prepare the meat sauce:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onion and cook until soft (about 3–4 min).
  3. Add garlic and stir for 30 seconds.
  4. Add ground meat; cook until browned.
  5. Stir in tomato paste and sauce.
  6. Season with salt, pepper, and herbs.
  7. Let it simmer for 15–20 minutes on low heat.

    Prepare the béchamel sauce:

  1. In a saucepan, melt butter over medium heat.
  2. Stir in flour and cook for 1 minute (to remove the raw taste).
  3. Gradually whisk in milk, stirring constantly until smooth.
  4. Add salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  5. Cook until thickened, then remove from heat.

    Assemble the lasagna:

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce first.
  3. Add lasagna sheets to cover the base.
  4. Add a layer of meat sauce, then a layer of béchamel, then cheese.
  5. Repeat layers until the dish is nearly full (usually 3–4 layers).
  6. Finish with béchamel and a generous layer of cheese on top.

    Bake:

  1. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  2. Remove foil and bake another 15–20 minutes until golden and bubbly.

    Rest & Serve:

  1. Let it rest for 10–15 minutes before cutting — this helps it set and slice neatly.
